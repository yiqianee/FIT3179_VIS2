Australian Airport Statistics ✈️ ☁️

Geographical Spread of Airports

Most of Australia’s major airports are concentrated along the eastern side, where population and economic activity are highest, including Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane. Each state also has a large-sized airport, such as Perth in the west and Darwin in the north, ensuring strong national connectivity.

Do you know airport have scores?

Sydney Kingsford Smith Airport has consistently achieved the highest overall score among all Australian airports.

The score is calulated based on a range of factors including airline and passenger surveys, as well as objective indicators such as such as on-time performance, flight delays, and passenger complaints.

Passenger Traffic Battle

Sydney and Melbourne are Australia’s busiest airports, buzzing with energy and activity year-round.

Nestled in the heart of highly populated regions, and as major economic and cultural centers, these airports naturally thrive as bustling hubs for both domestic and international flights, attracting a diverse mix of travelers and serving as prime gateways to Australia’s most iconic destinations. What sets these airports apart is their world-class infrastructures like multiple runways, spacious terminals, and seamless ground transportation.

From People to Packages

After analyzing passenger traffic, we now explore which airports handle the most cargo movements.

Sydney used to be the major hub for freight transport, managing about 45% of the national cargo. However, over the years, Melbourne has gradually taken over, becoming the largest hub for cargo volumes in Australia.
